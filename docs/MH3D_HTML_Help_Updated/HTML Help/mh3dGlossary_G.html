Ghost

A segment that does not render. A Ghost often works as a patriarch to coordinate the movements of seemingly independent Segments. For example, a Ghost could be used to link together letters in a word. Though each of the letters may rotate in different ways, moving the Ghost moves all of the letters together.

Global Ambiance

Raises the ambient level of the entire rendered scene.

Global Axis

The X, Y, or Z axis.

Global Coordinates

Used when you want a Material to not be connected to an object’s surface, but rather move along that surface independently. Fog would be an example. You could put a fog-like Material over the object, using global coordinates. This would give the appearance that the object was moving through the fog.

Global Dynamic Settings

The default settings for the dynamics functions. Edited in the Direction Preferences Dialog.

Gradient

A material combiner which creates a dithering effect between two material Nodes.

Ground Line

The line drawn for reference to indicate where the ground (or 0 Y value) is located.

Group

Control points that translate, scale, and rotate together.
