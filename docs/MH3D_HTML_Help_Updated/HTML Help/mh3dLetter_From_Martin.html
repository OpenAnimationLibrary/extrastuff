The road to professional animation is long and arduous. I too am traveling this road as any student familiar with the many releases of this software can attest. I have experienced the anger and frustration with computers just like all of you. It has given me a wry humor concerning "the evil little box". (Personally, I balance my checkbook with a pencil). If it was not for the images: those beautiful, fantastic, inspiring pictures that "the box" creates, I would abandon the whole hi-tech mess. But it's those pictures - pictures and animations I too can create even though my talents are meager and my creativity average. That's why I conduct an orchestra of coders whose symphony is this software. Their reward is the music of animation you customers create. Moreover, frustration aside, we all love what we are doing.

There is another side to this equation of aspiring animator and earnest coder. The computer animation industry is exclusionary due mostly to cost, but with this software you can directly compete with even the most exclusive studios. The sense of underdog accomplishment permeates every animation you create. There is a current of respect and curiosity that flows around this software; from the aspiring animators who use it and from professionals in the industry watching you. We coders work our hearts out to maintain the awe of the technologists, but only you can gain the respect of the artists.
