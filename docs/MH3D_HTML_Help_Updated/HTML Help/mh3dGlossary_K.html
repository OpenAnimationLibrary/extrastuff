Keyframes

The process of creating only the frames of an animation where the extremes of motion occur. For example, if an arm is to move up, the frame with the arm at its lowest extreme would be drawn, as well as the frame where the arm was at its uppermost extreme. The remaining frames which create the full motion are drawn in by the computer. This is sometimes referred to as ‘betweening’.

Kinematics

The chain created by the hierarchical organization of a character. Kinematics is what allows you to grab the tip of a figures finger and move the whole arm. The computer does all the calculations to make the entire movement happen.

Klieg

An omni-directional light type, selectable on the Attribute Panel.
