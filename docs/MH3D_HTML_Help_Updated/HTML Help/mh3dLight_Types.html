There are six types of light sources: Kliegs, Bulbs, and Suns - with and without shadows. Each light has channels associated with it, so that it is possible to change the light values over time.

To change a lights attributes, click on the light name in the Project Workspace. The Properties Panel will change and reflect the selected lights settings.

To change a light type, simply press the appropriate light type button. You will notice that the light will change in the Choreography window to reflect the new type.

Kliegs

Klieg lights are omnidirectional but their area of illumination is circumscribed by the cone defined by the width at the small end and the Focal Length at the large end. Distance to Fall-off defines where the light will begin fall-off. Fall-off also increases from the axis of the cone out to the surface of the cone.

Klieg lights are the only light type that has an option for using Shadow Maps, which speed up rendering, and provide softer looking shadows.

Bulbs

The default light type is Bulb. Bulbs are omnidirectional, meaning they cast light rays out as a sphere. A Bulb's intensity starts to decrease, or fall-off, at the Distance to Fall-off. Changes to the Focal length parameters have no effect on Bulbs.

Suns

Suns are unidirectional in the direction of the Sun's Light Target. Changes in Width, Focal length, and Distance have no effect on Suns. A Sun's intensity remains constant, meaning there is no fall-off.
