A “Kinematic” constraint lets a character hold onto a steering wheel as it turns, or a leg follow as you position a character’s feet walking up stairs.

Kinematic constraints are used to help hold a bone in place while other bones higher in the bone hierarchy are moved. Conversely, if the bones that are the object of the kinematic constraint are moved, the hierarchy of the model will translate accordingly.

In this example, the Thom character is holding onto the sphere. This was done by adding a Kinematic constraint to each forearm bone that was constrained to bones along the surface of the sphere.
