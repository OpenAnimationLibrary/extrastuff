Each window gives you a small peek into a larger universe that would be too cumbersome to manipulate if it were all visible at one time. The universe has boundaries that are virtually unlimited. Your windows can view any section of the total universe at almost any magnification, or zoom.

There are three ways to Zoom using the zoom button:

The first is to click the Zoom button on the Navigation toolbar, then press and hold the mouse button inside the appropriate window. Move the mouse left to decrease the magnification, and right to increase the magnification.

The second is to click the Zoom button, then press and hold the right mouse button inside the appropriate window. While holding the right mouse button down, drag a bounding box around the area you wish to see in more detail. When you release the mouse button, the area you surrounded in the bounding box will now fill the entire window.

The third way is to simultaneously click the Zoom button while you press the <Shift> key. The window will attempt to zoom its contents so that everything is visible into the current view. The default shortcut key for Zoom is Z.
