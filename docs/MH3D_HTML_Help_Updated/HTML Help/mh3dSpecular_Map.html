Specularity maps are another technique for adding a realistic touch to your models. Specularity maps are greyscale images. When applied to a model, dark areas on the Specularity map cause the specularity of the underlying object to lessen.

Completely black areas of the Specular map allow no surface specularity. If the amount of specularity varies across an object’s surface, if makes the surface appear as if it is not perfectly smooth, just like objects in real life are rarely perfectly smooth. Of course, the angle of the light causing the specular highlighting must be right for the effect to occur. Specularity maps override the base specularity of an object.

The value determines how much effect the grayscale image has on the surface. A higher number would make the specularity higher. Reasonable values for Specularity maps are from 1 to 1000%.
