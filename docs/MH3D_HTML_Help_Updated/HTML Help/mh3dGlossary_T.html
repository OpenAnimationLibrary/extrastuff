Templates

An image over which you can draw a similar model, like tracing over a picture. You can load a template in any window and then draw over it.

Target Rate

The maximum frames per second to keyframe an action when capturing a sequence from a motion capture device .

Targets

Functions which indicate what another object is directed toward.

Tiling

Repeating a map several times to cover an object.

Tilt

A camera movement in which the camera moves up and down.

Title Safe

The area of the camera view where it is best to keep titles in order to guarantee best visibility on a television. The Title Safe region falls within the TV Safe region.

Tool Panels

The grouping of tools available for the current part of the program.

Total Frames

The number of Frames it takes to complete a figures choreography. As keyframes are added, the number of Total Frames will increase.

Translation

A segments physical position with respect to other segments.

Transparency Maps

Image maps that allow an opaque surface to have various amounts of colored transparency. They use color intensity to indicate the amount of transparency on a pixel-by-pixel basis. The lower the intensity of the color, the greater the transparency. Totally black areas in the transparency map cut a hole in the object’s surface.

Turbulence

The distortion of the perfect edges when two material Nodes are combined to make them appear more organic or natural.

Turn View

Changes the angle of the view within the active window.

TV Safe

The area in which any action should be kept in order to guarantee best visibility on television.

Twin View

A common window layout showing two equally sized windows side by side(front view and top view.
