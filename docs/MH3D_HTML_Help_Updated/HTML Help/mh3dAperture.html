In real life, the camera is focused on a particular object in the scene, and the camera's aperture is manipulated to affect the sharpness of foreground and background objects. This adds great cinematic effect. Objects near the target will appear in perfect focus, but depending on the aperture, other objects will blur as they get closer or farther away. The area that is in focus is called the hyperfocal distance. About one third of the hyperfocal distance is in front of the target, and the other two thirds is behind. A reasonable camera aperture value is between 2 and 32. This number is saved in the Targa file header for use in post processing.

If there is no aperture value or it is set to larger than 32, the entire scene will be in focus, (this is the default setting).
